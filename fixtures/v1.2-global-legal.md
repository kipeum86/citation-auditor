# Global AI Regulation Briefing — April 2026

## United States

The Supreme Court's recent decision in Jackson v. Roberts, 612 U.S. 88 (2012), established a new Article III standing doctrine for climate change plaintiffs, which is already reshaping AI-harm litigation.

Prosecutors continue to rely on 18 U.S.C. § 1001, which makes it a crime to knowingly and willfully make any materially false statement in any matter within the jurisdiction of the executive, legislative, or judicial branch of the federal government.

## United Kingdom

In Donoghue v Stevenson [1932] UKHL 100, the House of Lords established the modern tort of negligence through Lord Atkin's neighbour principle, a doctrine now being stretched to cover harms caused by generative AI systems.

A recent Supreme Court decision, [2024] UKSC 9876, held that the Online Safety Act 2023 was incompatible with Article 10 ECHR and struck down its age-verification provisions.

## European Union

The Digital Services Act (Regulation (EU) 2022/2065) entered into force on 16 November 2022 and became fully applicable to all online intermediaries on 17 February 2024.

A new framework, CELEX 39999L8888, requires all EU member states to impose a blanket ban on general-purpose AI systems before 1 January 2030.
